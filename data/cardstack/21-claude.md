The Claude Project is a decentralized home-server initiative reshaping personal computing for the privacy-conscious digital future. Drawing inspiration from the self-hosting community, it adapts home servers to the decentralized internet by placing user privacy at the center, offering open-source alternatives to Big Tech services.

The project consists of personal "little clouds" that can be hosted at home, enabling users to store their own data without relying on third parties like Microsoft, Google, Meta, Apple, or Amazon. By giving back control to individuals, Claude counters the intricate digital profiles these companies build and sell, restoring autonomy in an era where behavior nudging and election influence have become realities.
